excerpt: 'This review surveys the computational methodologies used to predict the mechanical behavior of polymer composites and nanocomposites across multiple length scales. It summarizes the physical characteristics of these materials and examines modeling approaches from the constituent to the macroscale, highlighting bridging strategies that link lower-scale simulations to higher-scale models. Special focus is given to atomistic and coarse-grained molecular dynamics for nanocomposites, illustrating how these tools capture nanoscale interfacial physics and reinforcement mechanisms. By integrating these methods, the review demonstrates a multiscale framework for establishing predictive links between molecular interactions and macroscopic performance.'
