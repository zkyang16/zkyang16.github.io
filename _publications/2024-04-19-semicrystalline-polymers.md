title: "Unveiling the Nanoconfinement Effect on Crystallization of Semicrystalline Polymers Using Coarse-Grained Molecular Dynamics Simulations"
collection: publications
category: manuscripts
excerpt: 'Semicrystalline polymers under nanoconfinement exhibit distinct properties compared to bulk, yet the effects of nanoconfinement on local crystallization and structural evolution remain incompletely understood. Using coarse-grained molecular dynamics simulations, this study examines the crystallization of polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) under varying levels of nanoconfinement induced by implicitly represented nanoparticles. Results show that nanoconfinement promotes crystallization — particularly in early stages — with nanoparticle-polymer interfaces serving as nucleation sites. The degree of crystallinity generally increases with confinement level, and crystallization proceeds heterogeneously, with regions closer to interfaces crystallizing earlier and more extensively. These effects persist even under high cooling rates. The findings provide insights into the interplay between nanoconfinement and crystallization behavior, with implications for the design of semicrystalline polymer-based nanocomposites.'
