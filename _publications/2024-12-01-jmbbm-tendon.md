title: "Understanding the effects of mineralization and structure on the mechanical properties of tendon-bone insertion using mesoscale computational modeling"
collection: publications
category: manuscripts
excerpt: 'The tendon-bone insertion (enthesis) is a specialized interface that transfers forces while minimizing stress concentrations, featuring gradient mineralization and branching fiber structures. However, how these features at the mesoscale fiber level govern mechanical function remains poorly understood. This study develops mesoscale computational models of the enthesis, capturing branching fiber networks and varying mineralization scales, and performs tensile simulations to assess five mechanical properties: Young's modulus, strength, toughness, resilience, and failure strain. Results reveal that mineralization scale and branching angle geometry significantly influence mechanical properties, with tradeoffs emerging between mutually exclusive properties — notably, an optimal mineralization scale exists that maximizes toughness. Three distinct fracture regimes are identified that correspond to these tradeoffs. These findings deepen understanding of composition-structure-function relationships at the enthesis and offer guidance for the biomimetic design of fibrillar interfaces joining soft and hard materials.'
